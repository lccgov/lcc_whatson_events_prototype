Wheelchair users can access the rink in their own wheelchairs, and should be accompanied on ice by a responsible skater. Disabled users can book for any session although if a quieter session may be more appropriate (where more space is available), then please note these run at the following times:

Ice Rink Rules

For everyone’s safety and enjoyment, all skaters must make themselves aware of the following rules of the rink before going on the ice:

  • Skating can be a hazardous activity and is entirely at your own risk
  • Children aged 12yrs and under must be accompanied at the Ice Cube by an adult (aged 18yrs or over), and those aged 8yrs and under must be accompanied on the ice at all times by an adult
  • We have ten penguin stabilisers available to hire for £1 (cash) on a first-come first-served basis. Skating aids cannot be pre-booked. With a height restriction of 1.1m
  • Always skate in an anti-clockwise direction, unless directed otherwise by the Ice Manager
  • Be aware of and respectful to other skaters at all times
  • Do not spin, jump, speed skate or cause danger to others, so skate within your limitations
  • No bags, smoking, food and drink or the use of hoods, phones, personal music players are permitted whilst on the ice
  • No chains of more than three skaters
  • Never throw snow or ice, or chip or dig holes in the ice
  • Always follow instructions given by ice marshals and stewards

Please note any skating deemed dangerous to others will not be tolerated and failure to comply with the above rules may result in you being asked to leave

Skate Information

Children

Parents must judge whether their children can skate safely. All children aged 12 years and under must be accompanied at the rink by a parent or guardian aged 18 years or over. All children aged 8 years and under must be accompanied by a parent or guardian aged 18 years or over, who must have a ticket, and be with them on the ice and at all other times.

The popular ‘penguin skate stabilisers’, provides a safe haven for young children aged 8 years and under to find their feet (they must be accompanied by a parent or guardian aged 18 years or over, who must have a ticket, and be with them on the ice and at all other times).

Skate wear

Skate rental is included in your ticket price and sizes range from a child’s 8 to an adult’s 15, with double-bladed ‘bob skates’ for our youngest visitors. If you have your own pair of skates we’ll store your shoes for free. Safety helmets are available to hire (£1 per session payable at the rink), and are recommended for use for all children aged 12 years or under.

Lockers are also available for hire (£2 large/ £1 small, per session, payable at the rink).

Skaters are reminded that bags and using mobile phones, hoods, cameras or personal music players is not permitted on the ice. Socks, hats and gloves can be purchased at the rink, but remember to wrap up warm with layered clothing and waterproofs in wet weather.

First time skating

There's no need to worry if you have never skated before as our friendly team of Ice Marshals will be more than happy to give you tips and advice on how to stay on your feet. Just relax, slightly bend your knees, learn forward then push off with one foot and glide with the other...

Learn to skate with a four week group tuition programme or individual lessons. Places are limited so book early to avoid disappointment!

Ice Rink Rules

Our experienced Ice Marshals are on hand to promote a safe and enjoyable experience at all times. However, Ice skating can be a hazardous activity so please be aware that any on-ice activity is entirely at your own risk.

All skaters must make themselves aware of the ice rink rules before going on the ice, and follow the instructions and guidance of the Ice Marshals at all times.

Remember to be aware of, respectful and courteous to other skaters at all times!

Photography and filming

Using mobile phones and cameras is not allowed on the ice, however you can buy a memento of your skate from the professional photographer who will be at the rink during most public sessions.

The following photography and filming policy applies at the rink:

  • Recreational photography of family, friends and / or scenic views of the venue / event is permitted, except where other persons attending the event can be clearly identified as the main focus. Please therefore be respectful to other people’s privacy at all times or you may be challenged to stop and / or leave
  • No tripods or other large photography / film equipment, may be used in the venue without prior written authorisation
  • Photography / filming / recordings for professional / commercial use is not permitted without prior written authorisation
  • All persons entering the venue consent to any photography, filming and recording as arranged by the organisers and/or Leeds City Council for possible future transmission or promotional purposes

For further information please email events@leeds.gov.uk
