The Story of Lady Feilding

The Story of Lady Feilding during the First World War Presented by Julie Ann Cooper

After last years successful festival appearance Julie is back with a new one woman performance.

Lady Dorothie Feilding was the 24 year old daughter of the Earl of Denbigh, who volunteered her services to drive an ambulance during the First World War.

She worked for the Munro Corps, helping Belgian and French as well as British Troops, exposing herself to many dangers and risks.

This performance celebrates the 100th Anniversary of Lady Dorothie being awarded the Military Medal by King George V in 1916.

This event will take place in the Baker Room and is part of Morley Arts Festival.

Tickets £5. Available from Morley Library and on www.ticketsource.co.uk/leedslibraryevents
